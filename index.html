Project Reflection

Learning Journey & Key Takeaways

This project was a significant learning experience, pushing me to independently explore and master various aspects of game development. I started with a basic understanding of programming and ended with a functional 2D platformer, a testament to self-directed learning and continuous problem-solving.

  • Specific skills learned: I gained extensive experience in C# scripting for game logic, including player movement, animation states, and UI interactions. I learned to effectively use Unity's built-in physics system and how to manipulate it for custom jump behaviors. Furthermore, I became proficient in pixel art creation and animation using Aseprite, translating static designs into lively character and environmental sprites. I also learned to integrate and utilize Unity packages like DOTween for creating smooth, visually appealing transitions and animations.
  • Concepts grasped: I developed a strong understanding of the game loop and how various game elements interact within it. I delved into the intricacies of custom movement implementations versus relying solely on Unity's physics engine, understanding the trade-offs and benefits of each. The project also introduced me to basic UI/UX design principles as I built and refined the options and tutorial menus.
  • Unexpected discoveries: One surprising discovery was the complexity of seemingly simple features, such as implementing a robust wall climb/slide mechanic or ensuring consistent player hitbox interactions with different environments. I also realized the critical importance of regular version control and robust backup strategies after experiencing project corruption.

Challenges Faced & Solutions Implemented

Throughout the development, I encountered several challenges that tested my problem-solving skills, ultimately leading to a more resilient and refined project.

  • Major technical hurdles:
    • Custom Physics and Player Movement: Initially, creating a custom jump and movement system that felt responsive and natural was a significant hurdle (Week Two, Week Three). I struggled with inconsistent jump heights and awkward movements. Solution: I researched various platformer physics tutorials (e.g., "Improve your Platformerâ€™s Jump (and Wall Jump) | Unity", "Math for Game Programmers: Building a Better Jump"), experimented with different force applications, and iteratively fine-tuned parameters until the movement felt fluid. I also looked into how established platformers like Celeste handle their physics.
    • Animation Integration: Integrating pixel art animations from Aseprite into Unity and ensuring smooth transitions between states (idle, walk, jump, dash) proved challenging (Week Four). Solution: I spent considerable time learning Unity's Animator component, researching best practices for 2D animation controllers, and creating new scripts to manage animation states based on player input and actions.
    • Project Corruption: The project corruption in the "Week of May 1, 2025" was a major setback. Solution: I relied heavily on my last working backup, meticulously comparing files to identify what was lost and systematically rebuilding the affected parts, particularly the UI elements and script linkages (Week of May 5 - May 9). This experience highlighted the need for more frequent and disciplined backups.
    • Level End Chip Functionality: Integrating the level end chip, including audio, particle effects, and scene transitions, presented multiple debugging challenges (Week of May 12 - May 16, Week of May 19 - May 23). Solution: I systematically debugged the issue by checking variable declarations, collider settings, and build settings. I used debug logs to confirm code execution and meticulously assigned all required components (Particle Systems, Audio Clips, UI Images) in the Unity Inspector.
  • Design challenges: Designing intuitive levels that gradually introduced mechanics while maintaining engagement was tricky. I drew inspiration from games like Celeste for level flow. Creating visually appealing pixel art that fit the steampunk theme and ensuring consistent scaling across different resolutions were also ongoing considerations.
  • How you overcame them: My primary method for overcoming challenges was iterative development, coupled with extensive research. I utilized online tutorials, Unity documentation, and community forums to understand concepts and troubleshoot issues. I broke down complex problems into smaller, manageable tasks and tested frequently. Gathering feedback, even informal, also helped refine gameplay.
  • What you would do differently: If I faced the project corruption again, I would implement more frequent and robust version control practices from the start, such as using Git more diligently for smaller, more frequent commits. I would also establish a clearer testing protocol earlier in the development cycle to catch bugs before they compound.

Successes & Accomplishments

Despite the challenges, I achieved several key successes that I am particularly proud of, showcasing my growth as a game developer.

  • Favorite features: I am most satisfied with the player character's movement and dash mechanics. The implementation of custom physics for the jump and the visual aftereffects for the dash (Week Five) made the character feel responsive and fun to control. The level end chip's visual and audio feedback, combined with the smooth transition using DOTween (Week of May 26 - May 30), also turned out very well.
  • Personal milestones: Successfully implementing the core player movement (jump, run, wall slide/climb) was a major milestone. Getting the UI systems (options menu, tutorial HUD) to function correctly after the project corruption was also a significant achievement. Creating my own pixel art assets in Aseprite and seeing them come to life in Unity was incredibly rewarding.
  • Meeting project goals: I successfully developed a functional 2D platformer demonstrating key game development principles and showcasing my ability to learn and apply new technologies. The project effectively demonstrates my understanding of game design, programming, and asset integration, aligning with the objectives of a personal interest course.

Future Improvements & Iterations

This project serves as a strong foundation, and there are many avenues for future expansion and refinement.

  • Next steps for the game:
    • More Levels and Mechanics: I would design and implement more diverse levels, introducing new traps, enemies, and interactive environmental elements to increase gameplay variety.
    • Sound Design Polish: While basic audio is present, a dedicated pass for more dynamic sound effects and a more engaging background music system would significantly enhance the player experience.
    • Additional Player Abilities: Implementing new player abilities, such as a double jump, different types of dashes, or even basic combat mechanics, would add depth.
    • Main Menu and Pause Menu: Fully fleshing out a robust main menu with level selection and a pause menu with comprehensive options (beyond graphics settings) would improve usability.
  • Skills to further develop: I aim to deepen my understanding of advanced C# scripting patterns for cleaner and more modular code, explore more complex animation systems, and delve into performance optimization techniques for Unity games. I also want to learn more about game analytics to better understand player behavior and further refine game design.
  • Refined processes: Based on this experience, I would dedicate more time to pre-production planning and prototyping, especially for core mechanics, to minimize rework. I would also seek user feedback earlier and more frequently throughout the development cycle to guide design decisions and ensure a more engaging player experience.
